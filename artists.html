Artists

Beauty transcends norms, convention and predictability. Whilst the brain lights up when we view art we enjoy, people will be moved by different things. Artists often have a way of creating images that unexpectedly evoke a sense of pleasure. Emerging brain research proves what artists and art lovers have sensed all along: Art can make us feel better. You don’t need to be a brain scientist to understand that the arts have physiological, psychological, and spiritual beneﬁts.

Artists have always known that the arts change us in profound ways. The added part is that because of noninvasive technology allowing us to get inside our heads, we’re understanding more neurobiology at a detailed level. And the only reason that matters is so we can create better solutions using the arts for personalised prescriptions, ﬁne-tuning what to dose and dosages, and understanding how to apply art forms for healing.

Curiosity, surprise, wonder — all attributes found in art for the maker or the beholder — these are really important for human development. Researchers are ﬁnding that we as humans are hard-wired for aesthetic experiences. The arts are not just fundamental aspects of our humanity, but also essential to our well-being.

We honour the many different kinds of beauty with An Eye for Beauty, and the artists that create vast worlds for us to connect with, and experience.

Sohni Farrukh


Sohni is a lawyer who left the profession to pursue her passion for the arts. Her miniatures are modern and whimsical despite their resemblance to some of the great miniatures of the Mughal Empire. Her ability to create intricate and vast worlds of beauty is undeniable.


Qazi Shaharyar


Qazi's dervish dancers are dancing amongst the calligraphy he has mastered. A wall of his work can transport you to a very pristine and beautiful place. His use of colour and his knack for blending elements invites one to embrace tradition coming together with modernity.


Hafsa Zuberi


Hafsa has a gift for realism and storytelling through her art. Often depicting people with traditional style she has a modern perspective that shines through in her work. Her unique choices are an ode to her experience with self portraits and cultural symbolism. The beauty we see in her work is as unique as each individual.


Momina Shah


Momina has a knack for making everyday scenes of Pakistan come to life with an enigmatic style. Her work invites the viewer to appreciate culture and society through a provocative lens which inspires inquisition. Moreover the way she honours people of her home country encourages love and appreciation, an ode to the beauty all around us.


Emaan Koreshi


Emaan is an artist of a rare breed. She is self taught and draws inspiration from an array of the spiritual and cultural aspects of the human experience. Her work is rich with divine symbolism showcasing the beauty one can ﬁnd inside their own head. With great technical skill she creates a breathtaking world of ethereal beauty.


Serene Aneeq


Serene has a unique style of eerie suburban landscapes and more notably her dot work creations of absurd and strange symbols. Beauty is deﬁned in each of her artworks as something transcendent of expectations and norms. With divine symbolism and great technical skill she invites the viewer to question their ideal of Beauty.
Haiqa Ghazi


Haiqa is an exceptionally skilled artist with a knack for making the world see beauty in the darkest of times. Her work is often strange and eerie touching on darker themes, whilst her sheer talent allows people to expand their ideals of beauty.


Shanaya Khan


Shanaya is an artist with a unique style. She creates a mixture of people, landscapes and some abstract works too. She plays on identity and human emotion with her symbolism and uses colour in a seemingly calculated way. Her work reminds us of the beauty found in even the intense emotions people experience.


Maheen Amer


Maheen is a graphic artist emerging at a young age. Her art contains images of warmth and culture with elements she handpicks to exhibit her modern ﬂare for design. Each piece tells a story and invites the viewer to love and appreciate the beauty around us.


Zainab Hussain


Zainab has created a strange and beautiful world of her own with her digital drawings. Her work is able to transport the viewer to a land where beauty and absurdity come together in harmonious squiggles and holographic colours. A beautiful insight into a modern take on what inspires humanity to keep going.
